Fall 2023 Concert Series

Our Fall 2023 Concert Series is here!

Our Mainstage Series gives you the opportunity to hear performances from our internationally acclaimed faculty. Our Ensemble Series showcases the variety of exciting ensembles at the School of Music. In our Noon Series, you can enjoy free performances from the School of Music community during your lunch hour!

 

Ticket prices: 

Mainstage Series: $26 (General)/$20 (Students & Seniors)/$5 (12 & under)

Ensemble Series: $22 (General)/$18 (Students & Seniors)/$5 (12 & under)

Noon Series: FREE

All ticket prices include fees.

 

***Road closures on Elizabeth Avenue***
Please note that there are several detours currently in effect due to the temporary closure of a section of Elizabeth Avenue due to construction.
See here for a map for the latest detours as of Nov 6, 2023.

Let My Love Be Heard
Memorial University Choirs
ENSEMBLE SERIES
Saturday, November 18, 12pm ***New time!***
D.F. Cook Recital Hall

Only Light
Wind Ensemble
ENSEMBLE SERIES
Saturday, October 21, 1:30pm
D.F. Cook Recital Hall

Embark on a radiant journey through the captivating realm of light as the Memorial University Wind Ensemble presents a concert that illuminates the senses. The Music of Dialect: A Compositional Tribute to the Speech and Stories of Newfoundland and Labrador
Steve Cowan and Jason Noble
MAINSTAGE SERIES
Thursday, September 14, 7:30pm
D.F. Cook Recital Hall

Guitarist Steve Cowan and composer Jason Noble return home to present a concert of all-original music composed in honour of Newfoundland and Labrador.  Recordings of interviews with residents of the province are interwoven with guitar music, producing an enchanting soundworld somewhere between speech and music. The pieces touch on themes close to the hearts of many Newfoundlanders including community identity, Beaumont Hamel, ghost and fairy stories, kitchen parties, and change over time. Featuring the Newfoundland premiere of Noble's internationally acclaimed Folk Suite, with guest performer Michelle Cheramy on flute. $26/$20 (fees included) 

 

All About That Bass
Vernon Regehr, Meredith Johnson, Ema Shiroma, Daniel Fuchs, and Patrick Cashin
MAINSTAGE SERIES
Wednesday, October 18, 7:30pm
D.F. Cook Recital Hall

We are thrilled to welcome back double bassist Meredith Johnson to the Music at Memorial concert stage after almost a decade! He’ll be anchoring a program of chamber music that features Parlour Games, a new work for cello and bass by Michael Oesterle, performed with cellist Vernon Regehr. Intended to be played in whatever order the performers wish, these 12 short movements are light-hearted and fun, and are based on the concept that Bela Bartok used in his duo for 2 violins. The rest of the program is rounded out by another work for bass and cello entitled Duo di Basso by Czech composer Zdenek Lukas, and Ralph Vaughan Williams’ early work, the sumptuous piano quintet.  $26/$20 (fees included) 

The Ecstasy of Becoming
Flying Pooka!
MAINSTAGE SERIES
Thursday, October 5, 7:30pm
D.F. Cook Recital Hall

Flying Pooka! is the duo of saxophonist and vocalist Dani Oore and pianist Florian Hoefner. Formed during the pandemic during Oore's time as postdoc at MUN the two are now launching their debut album "The Ecstasy of Becoming". Whenever they play, all music is made up in the moment. Their kinship and synergy explodes in exuberant melodious rhythmic improvisations with harmonic sophistication and structural depth. Raw, elegant, bold, fragile …and maybe playful. $26/$20 (fees included)
