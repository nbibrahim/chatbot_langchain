Profiles of Graduate Students

Kori Andrea

Ph.D. Candidate

Kerton Group

Kori completed her B.Sc. at Cape Breton University with a focus on functionalized nanoparticles for biomedical applications and pursued summer research in Germany via the DAAD RISE program. She received an NSERC Canada Graduate Scholarship (CGS-M) scholarship to pursue her research in the Kerton group and the prestigious NSERC Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship for her doctoral studies.

She is developing new catalysts for the transformation of carbon dioxide into organic molecules and polymers. In her free time, Kori enjoys travelling and playing sports.

  1. Andrea, K.A.; Brown, T.R.; Murphy, J.M.; Jagota, D.; McKearney, D.; Kozak, C.M.; Kerton F.M. Inorg. Chem. 2018 57, 13494 DOI: 10.1021/acs.inorgchem.8b02109
  2. Andrea, K.A.; Wang, L.;  Carrier A. J.; Campbell, M.; Buhariwalla, M.; MacKenzi, M., MacQuarrie, S. L.; Bennett, C.; Mkandawire, M., Oakes, K.; Lu, M.;  Zhang X.
    Langmuir  2017 33 (16), 3926
    DOI: 10.1021/acs.langmuir.6b04172

Ernest Awoonor-Williams

Ph.D. Candidate

Rowley Group

Ernest completed his B.Sc. (Hons) at Mount Allison University in New Brunswick, Canada. He joined the Rowley computational chemistry group in 2014. He is using computer simulations to aid the development of a new type of anti-cancer drugs.

In 2017, he received the prestigious NSERC Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship.

  1.  Awoonor-Williams, E., Walsh, A. G., Rowley, C. N. Modeling covalent-modifier drugs BBA Proteins and Proteom. 2017, doi: 10.1016/j.bbapap.2017.05.009

Azam Sayadi

Ph.D. Candidate 

Pickup Group

Azam completed her B.Sc. and my M.Sc. in Bu-Ali- Sina University of Hamedan, Iran. In September 2015, she joined Professor Peter Pickup’s research group at Memorial University. She is currently working on developing reliable and easy methodologies for evaluation of catalytic electrooxidation of organic fuels. She enjoys painting, hiking and cooking in my free time.

  1. A Sayadi, P. G. Pickup, Evaluation of methanol oxidation catalysts by rotating disc voltammetry, Electrochimica Acta, 2016, doi: 10.1016/j.electacta.2016.03.112
  2. A Sayadi, P. G. Pickup, Evaluation of ethanol oxidation catalysts by rotating disc voltammetry, Electrochimica Acta, 215, 2016, 84

Farshid Shahrokhi

Ph.D. Candidate Yuming Zhao Group 

  

Farshid completed his M.Sc. at Mazandaran University, Babolsar, Iran. He joined Yuming Zhao’s research group at Memorial University of Newfoundland in September 2016. Farshid is currently working on the synthesis of new dendritic DTFs and TTFVs as a sensor for detecting nitroaromatics. Farshid enjoys traveling, fishing, swimming, and hiking. His hometown is Tehran, Iran.
