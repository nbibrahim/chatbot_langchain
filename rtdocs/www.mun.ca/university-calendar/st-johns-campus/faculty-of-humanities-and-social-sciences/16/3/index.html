    16.3.1 Greek

    All sections of the Greek language courses below, except CLAS 4999, follow the Language Study Course Guidelines available at www.mun.ca/hss/ls.

    Back to Top
    CLAS 1130 Introductory Ancient Greek I
    Content identifier: ARTS-5654

    familiarizes students with the basics of the Ancient Greek language. Students will master the Ancient Greek alphabet, learn how to read simple narratives in Ancient Greek, and examine the connections between language and culture. Evaluation will focus largely on comprehension of written Ancient Greek. All sections of this course follow the Language Study Course Guidelines available at www.mun.ca/hss/ls.

    EQ:

    Medieval and Early Modern Studies 1130, the former Medieval Studies 1130

    Back to Top
    CLAS 1131 Introductory Ancient Greek II
    Content identifier: ARTS-5655

    continues to familiarize students with the Ancient Greek language. Students will acquire a broad vocabulary, learn to read more complex passages of prose and poetry, and gain insights into key social concepts through study of language. All sections of this course follow the Language Study Course Guidelines available at www.mun.ca/hss/ls.

    EQ:

    Medieval and Early Modern Studies 1131, the former Medieval Studies 1131

    Content identifier: ARTS-5656

Students will read selections from works of history, literature, philosophy and oratory in Ancient Greek. All sections of this course follow the Language Study Course Guidelines available at www.mun.ca/hss/ls.

    16.3.2 Greek and Roman Studies

    Medieval and Early Modern Studies 3000 may be substituted for a Greek and Roman Studies course in both the Classics degree programs (Honours, Joint Honours and general degree) and the Greek and Roman Studies degree programs (Honours, Joint Honours and general degree).

    Back to Top
    CLAS 1001 Critical Reading and Writing: Classics in Popular Culture
    Content identifier: ARTS-11060

    is an introduction to the ways in which modern popular culture represents and understands the ancient Greek and Roman world. Emphasis is placed on learning and practising critical reading and writing skills, including the comprehension and analysis of primary sources and secondary literature, and effective academic composition. All sections of this course follow Critical Reading and Writing Course Guidelines available at www.mun.ca/hss/crw.
